Hello, I'm

Hsiao, Chun-En (蕭群恩)

Undergraduate Researcher · AI Security & VLA

I am an undergraduate student in the Arete Honors Program at National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University (NYCU), majoring in Artificial Intelligence in Engineering and Science.

I am currently an undergraduate research student (Undergraduate Researcher) at the BASIC Lab, advised by Prof. Hong-Han Shuai (帥宏翰). Our work includes investigating transferable adversarial attacks across heterogeneous object detection architectures, reducing hallucination in edge LLM tool calling through metacognitive verification, exploring vision-language-action model reasoning, and developing embedded AI systems for real-time vehicle perception on edge devices.

My research is guided by a fundamental curiosity: as AI systems become more capable and autonomous, how do we ensure they behave predictably under adversarial pressure, distribution shift, and real-world uncertainty? I believe that building trustworthy AI requires understanding failure modes from multiple angles — from probing the vulnerabilities of vision and language models through adversarial attacks, to studying how vision-language-action models reason and generalize in embodied settings. This cross-cutting perspective drives my work across AI security, natural language processing, computer vision, and embodied AI.
