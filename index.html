I am an undergraduate student in the Arete Honors Program at National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University (NYCU), majoring in Artificial Intelligence in Engineering and Science.

I am currently an undergraduate research student (專題生) at the BASIC Lab, advised by Prof. Hong-Han Shuai (帥宏翰). Our work includes investigating transferable adversarial attacks across heterogeneous object detection architectures, reducing hallucination in edge LLM tool calling through metacognitive verification, exploring vision-language-action model reasoning, and developing embedded AI systems for real-time vehicle perception on edge devices.

My research interests span AI security and robustness, computer vision, and vision-language-action models. I am broadly interested in understanding and improving the reliability of deep learning systems under adversarial conditions.

Reducing Residual Hallucination in Edge LLM Tool Calling (MetaCog)

BASIC Lab · Advised by Prof. Hong-Han Shuai (帥宏翰)

Mar. 2026 – Present
  • Investigating metacognitive verification in edge LLMs (<30B) for tool calling under information insufficiency, discovering that models possess latent parameter-verification capability but fail to activate it spontaneously.
  • Built MetaCog-Bench, a cross-domain benchmark (22 domains, 4,567 samples) for evaluating tool-calling hallucination and metacognition.
  • Developing R-Tuning (SFT + GRPO) to internalize verification behavior, achieving significant hallucination rate reduction on vanilla prompts without inference-time scaffolding.
